Training volume

Definition first, then interpretation.

What training volume means

In LiftShift, “volume” refers to the amount of work you performed, derived from your logged sets. A common representation is weight × reps summed across sets.

What changes volume

  • More sets
  • More reps per set
  • More load (weight)

How to interpret volume trends

  • A rising trend usually indicates higher training stress (assuming similar exercise selection).
  • Large spikes can be due to one unusually long session or a change in exercise mix.
  • Compare volume with consistency (how often you trained) to avoid misreading noisy weeks.
